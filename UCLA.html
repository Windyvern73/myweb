Overview

As a Student Researcher with the UCLA Elegant Mind Club in 2024, I worked under Professor Katsushi Arisaka to design and build a fully autonomous ground vehicle. The car used the MIDAS depth-perception model for real-time distance estimation, paired with ultrasonic sensors and onboard object detection to navigate and follow a person without manual control.

Beyond the autonomous car, I contributed to two additional experiments for Professor Arisaka's research into human sensory perception — building a reaction-time testing rig with the ESP32 to gather psychophysics data, and redesigning the UCLA Physics Department's harmonic oscillation lab experiment using the same microcontroller to modernise data collection.

The experience deepened my understanding of how computer vision, embedded systems, and robotics intersect — and gave me hands-on exposure to collaborative academic research at the university level.

Highlights

Autonomous Vehicle

Designed and built a prototype autonomous car that uses ML computer vision depth perception (MIDAS) and Arduino motor control to follow a person hands-free.

Binocular Disparity & SLAM

Collaborated with Prof. Arisaka to explore binocular disparity and 3D environment mapping using SLAM — bridging computer vision theory with real embedded hardware.

ESP32 Experiments

Built two independent ESP32-based rigs: a psychophysics reaction-time tester and a modernised harmonic oscillation experiment for the UCLA Physics Department.
