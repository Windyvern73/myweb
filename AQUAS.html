About the Project

Clearing New York's waterways, buoy by buoy

AQUAS (Aquatic Quality Unmanned Autonomous Systems) is CU Robotics' environmental initiative to combat harmful algae blooms in New York-area waterways. As an Electrical/Software Developer, I design and build the electronics that power our autonomous surface buoys — from the onboard Arduino microcontrollers to the cellular IoT modules that beam data back to shore.

Each buoy carries sensors for water temperature, turbidity, and chlorophyll concentration. The Arduino firmware I wrote handles sensor polling, threshold alerting, and wireless data transmission via SIM-based network transfer — so the team gets live readings from anywhere in the city without being on the water.

The project sits at the intersection of environmental science, embedded systems, and IoT networking — exactly the kind of hands-on, real-world engineering I came to Columbia to pursue.
