Field Notes

Building for the AIAA Competition

As an Electrical and Mechanical Designer for the CU Airplane Club, I design and fabricate components for Columbia's entry in the AIAA Design/Build/Fly competition — one of the most rigorous student aircraft competitions in the country. My work sits at the boundary between mechanical design and embedded electronics, translating competition requirements into physical hardware on the plane.

One of my key contributions has been designing a banner release mechanism using Fusion360 — a compact, reliable system that deploys a banner mid-flight as part of the competition scoring mission. I also designed a fuse-holder for the plane's electrical system, ensuring safe and accessible power management for the onboard electronics under flight conditions.

Working on a competition aircraft means every component needs to be both light enough to fly and robust enough to survive repeated testing cycles. It's pushed me to think carefully about design trade-offs between weight, strength, and manufacturability — skills that bridge my work in CAD with real hands-on fabrication.
